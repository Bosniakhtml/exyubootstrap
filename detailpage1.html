"Que Vadis, Aida?" is a haunting and thought-provoking war drama directed by acclaimed filmmaker Jasmila Žbanić. Set during the brutal Bosnian War in the 1990s, the film tells the harrowing tale of Aida, a skilled translator working for the United Nations at the UN base in the town of Srebrenica. As tensions escalate and the war ravages the region, Aida's world is turned upside down. She desperately tries to ensure the safety of her husband and two sons, who, like many others, have sought refuge in the UN compound, hoping for protection from the impending horrors of war. The film vividly portrays the chaos and desperation that engulfed Srebrenica as the Serbian army advances, leading to the infamous Srebrenica genocide. "Que Vadis, Aida?" offers an intimate and unflinching look at the anguish, fear, and moral dilemmas faced by Aida as she navigates the bureaucratic complexities of the UN and confronts the heartbreaking choices she must make to safeguard her family. The film's compelling narrative is complemented by exceptional performances, particularly by Jasna Đuričić, who portrays Aida with depth and emotional intensity. Žbanić's masterful direction and the film's stunning cinematography immerse viewers in the stark reality of war, making it impossible to turn away from the tragedy unfolding on screen. Ultimately, "Que Vadis, Aida?" is a profoundly moving and essential cinematic experience that sheds light on the dark chapter of the Bosnian War. It serves as a stark reminder of the devastating consequences of conflict, the fragility of human life, and the enduring strength of the human spirit in the face of unimaginable adversity. Show less
